Why this wedge works

Lead with throughput and resilience. Keep advanced solvers behind the scenes for later.

1

Start where planning pain is highest

Job-shop scheduling, machine assignment, shift constraints, and rework risk create combinatorial complexity that planners still manage with spreadsheets and tribal knowledge.

2

Deliver value before deep integrations

CSV and manual uploads make pilots fast. Customers can model one constrained line before committing to ERP or MES integrations.

3

Use deterministic optimization first

Start with explainable rules, bottleneck ranking, and clear constraint handling. Future quantum or LLM assistance can be layered on later.
